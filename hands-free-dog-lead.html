Hands-Free Dog Lead

Advanced Design Principles

What is the Hands-free lead?

The hands-free lead is essentially a waist belt with external attachments (metal rings) where the dog leads can be attached to.

Live Client - Wilko

Wilko gave me a task to design a product for the pet market which can eventually be featured in their stores. I was given 6 week to complete the task consisting of first and second hand research, market segmentation, testing, prototyping, 3D modelling and a culmination consisting a professional presentation.

 

The concept

A mechanism plays  a role of a simple exoskeleton-like feature. It is attached to the inside of the belt lead, so that the mechanism can lay flat on the lower back of the person wearing it. Moreover, the addition of an elastic silicon tube to the mechanism  acts as a shock absorbing system by keeping the pressure away from the spine.

 

Project Goal

 

 

During the research phase of the project, the data have shown that people using belt leads very often struggle with back pain caused by the frequent use of the belt and the high amount of pressure applied to the lower back.

Thus, these products must be focused especially on safety, comfort, and reliability. They have to withstand extreme pull forces, which can also cause serious back pain and even injuries. The belt must be centred around high-quality materials and great levels of comfort and safety. This goal was achieved with:

 

 

 

 

-the right choice of sustainable materials

-a maximum level of comfort and ergonomics

-modern, stylish, and practical design

-a new development (internal or external) that can prevent back injuries

-being suitable for everyone

-washable (water resistant if possible)

 

Ideas Generation

The mechanism plays a role of a simple exoskeleton-like feature. It is attached to the inside of the belt so that the mechanism can lay flat on the lower back of the person wearing it. Moreover, the addition of an elastic silicon tube to the mechanism will act as a shock absorbing system by keeping the pressure away from the spine. Overall, the inclusion of the exoskeletal mechanism and the elastic silicon tubes aid people in keeping a correct posture when wearing the belt. Therefore, the users will feel less back pain.

As part of the design process real testing is essential. In order to prove the efficiency of my design, the belt was put on a test with larger dog breeds. The provided data helped the project as giving it clear direction for changes improvement.

 

Solidworks provides exceptionally accurate opportunities for testing material behaviour and durability. Therefore, switching to a 3D software was a vital step as evidence of the physical test rigs and prototypes must be supported with an objective verification.
