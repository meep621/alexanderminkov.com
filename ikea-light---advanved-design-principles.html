In this project, I was asked to design a light fitting primarily for a domestic setting intended for an indoor use. The design is based around some given components from an existing IKEA light - a simple light bulb holder with a cable, switch and plug attached. The key target of the project is the personality of the product and the way in which it doest something interesting with the light. The project combined exploration of materials, key technologies, legislation and regulations, potential light sources, experimentations, multiple test rig tests,  and of course, a critical reflection of the design form a technical and functional point of view. In addition to that, things such as - target market, price point, assembly, electrical safety, materials and their associated manufacturing methods as well as packaging had also to be considered.

The light has three main components: a base structure, a top structure that fits in the base structure and four shades.

 

The colour plays an incredibly important role and can dramatically change the levels of illumination. Depending on how light or dark the colours of the materials are, the light will spread differently in the space.

 

The principle is simple: pull-strings are attached from the bottom of the shades to four hooks that are mounted to the top structure. When the top part is pulled upwards and twisted clockwise from the base, the pressure that is applied to the strings makes all the shades lift simultaneously. Once the desired form of the lamp is reached, the top part needs to be pushed back down, so it locks in.

 

191 bits were used for the physical build of the light. Several mothods were applied including - laser cutting, spray paining, stem bending and many others.
