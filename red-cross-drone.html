Module: Professional Design Practice

Project Length: 15 days

The concept of the Red Cross "Emotional relief drone" is to reach victims of natural disasters such as forest fires, floods and earthquakes. The drone is send by the red cross to the victims to spread awareness that people or supplies are coming soon. It is equipped with a camera, microphone and a small projector.

 

Idea From a Simple Sketch

 

 

The first days of the project were spent in researching drone trends, features, pricing range, brands etc. Some scenarios where outlined to short-list the possible features the drone must carry with it in order to be ‘‘The Red Cross Drone’’.

This concept was chosen from a small variety of design concepts developed during the first few days of the project. Decision was made to further develop this particular design proposal due to the well-planned shape of the main body which appealingly accommodates all the components needed. Perhaps, the primary ground this design was selected for further progression was the folding arm’s feature which seemed to fit the brief of the project.

 

Once the design concept was considered a deadline was set to complete each component of the final poster seven days before the deadline. In this case, I had seven days of tailoring and refining the poster. Thanks to that, more time was spent in finding a better way to deliver the message which resulted in the generation of not one but two posters.

 

 

 

At the beginning of the project, I found about a collaboration between the Austrian Red Cross and Land Rover in this project called ‘‘HERO’’. The drone is controlled using tablet app which allows the people in the car to explore the surrounding area. The drone follows the car as it drives while providing the people inside the car with live 360 views from above. On the top of the vehicle is located the landing dock with innovative side hatches and a pull-out floor. I manage to adopt and improve some of the features and ideas of this project.

The done would be extremely useful in areas with limited or no internet connection. Nowadays, reporting an emergency can be as simple as a social media post to #Red Cross and immediate response can be expected in minutes. However, in some area around the globe, a not consistent internet connection can sometimes be the changing factor in an emergency situation where action must be taken as quickly as possible.

This is where the our drone concept would make the difference. In a disaster situation, the first thing that spreads among people is panic and fear, especially when people are isolated from the outside world not knowing what is happening. The ability of the drone to project messages and let the victims know that they are not forgotten or alone is where this compact high tech piece shines.

Instead of having four axis for each arm, the drone has two vertical positioned tubes holding two electrical motors. Thanks to that, the arms can be fouled and the drone can be easily carried in a bag-pack.

On the front, small HD camera is being controlled by the rescue team to spot and locate human activity. The camera is also equipped with thermosensors in order to find people trapped under an avalanche or even ruins.
