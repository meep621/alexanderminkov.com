Speaker project

Module: Design Principles (2016)

aku is a wireless bluetooth speaker. It combines raw wool textile and cold aluminium frame exhibiting Nordic luxury and craftsmanship.

 

This module aims to help develop a wide range of design methodologies. By using applicable communication techniques, such as photographs and a physical visit of the venue, it makes it possible to acquire a better knowledge and understanding of the historical influences upon the design. Through establishing important analytical skills and developing a creative approach, one can learn to recognize and advance the relationship between design research and practice work. In consequence of learning to look closely at things, one will be able to develop a deeper understanding of the significance of form, detail, and function. Moreover, analysing and reflecting on a wide range of objects and places.

aku adopts a compact and functional form by using straight lines and highlighting the round edges of the loudspeaker. The curved edges display an elegant soft finish to the aluminium frame that holds all the pieces together.

aku. Magazine  Article (2016)

A symbolic approach through the process marks an evident minimalistic standpoint. As not visible control buttons are left, the volume controls are integrated within the environment. The port slots on the back of the speaker are also methodically accumulated in a compact easy to access area.

The natural wool textile exhibit pure cold pastel combination of colours which is being accompanied with a stylish aluminium frame surrounding the curved edges and promoting the product's cosy outlook.
